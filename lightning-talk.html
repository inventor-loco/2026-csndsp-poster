Energy-Autonomous Optical Camera Communication Node for Intra-Satellite Links

PresentingVicente Matus , IDeTIC, Universidad de Las Palmas de Gran Canaria
Jaime Aranda · Rafael Perez-Jimenez · Jose Rabadan
IDeTIC, ULPGC · PCT Tafira 35017, Las Palmas, Spain · vicente.matus@ulpgc.es
1
Problem Statement
  • Intra-satellite links are still mostly wired harnesses: added mass, poor modularity, and costly re-qualification whenever a subsystem moves.[1]
  • Optical Camera Communication (OCC) reuses ordinary LEDs as transmitters and cameras as receivers, but transmitter energy is ignored, assumed permanently powered.[2]
  • Solar cells have served as VLC receivers that harvest while decoding[3]; the reverse, a transmitter on harvested optical energy, remains unproven for spacecraft interiors.
Node architecture. Illumination → PV cell → ultracapacitor drives an MCU + LED transmitter; bursts reach the camera receiver. No power-bus connection.
2
Key Contributions of this Work

An energy-autonomous OCC transmitter node, powered solely by harvested optical energy and validated on a laboratory demonstrator.

  • Photovoltaic harvesting stored in a 5 F ultracapacitor drives threshold-triggered bursts (ON ≈ 3.3 V, OFF ≈ 3.0 V); first adaptation of autonomous OCC[4] to intra-satellite links.
  • Wireless for power & data: free placement inside the spacecraft, with no harness routing or re-qualification.
  • Camera spatial segregation lets many autonomous nodes coexist without cross-talk.
Key insight: energy availability, not modulation, governs effective throughput, motivating energy-aware OCC design.
Implemented system. Dark-room breadboard: an LED lamp illuminates the PV cell; the Arduino LED transmitter emits OOK bursts captured by the camera. Aluminium tape emulates a satellite panel.
